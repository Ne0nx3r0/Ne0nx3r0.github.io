YOU ARE NOT SAFE

( And that's okay )

"How secure should <thing> be?"
Many would say "totally secure!"
( That outta do it! )
But that's not possible because security has costs:
Time
Money
Convenience
Capability
Freedom
Which means...
Perfect security will cost you ALL
your time, money, conveniences,
capabilities and freedoms.
So what do we do?
We make trade-offs, everyday:
Visit a public place,
risk getting the flu
Drive to work,
risk car accident
Work around the house,
risk injuring yourself
Fly in a plane,
risk it crashing
Swim in the ocean,
risk shark attack
The last two probably sound the scariest

People are intuitively bad at estimating risk
They exaggerate rare risks...
"Did you hear about the shooting?"
...while downplaying common ones
"Pretty sure the circuit breaker is turned off"
They percieve personified risks...
"I saw those towers go down."
...as greater than anonymous risks
"I'd never fall for a social engineering attack."
And they struggle to estimate risks for unfamiliar situations
"Traveling to other countries is dangerous."
So what do we do?
Unplug your computer.

Hide under your bed.

Call 911 if anyone knocks at your door.
Okay but seriously.
Security is a trade off that has costs
Humans emotionally assess risk
Being secure and feeling secure
are two different things
It's okay to be concerned about risks,
but ask yourself if the costs are really worth the benefits.
You'll never be 100% safe.
And that's okay.
